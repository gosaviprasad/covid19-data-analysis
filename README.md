📖 Project Description
This project analyzes COVID‑19 case and vaccination data in India using Python, Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn, and Plotly.
It combines two datasets — one tracking confirmed cases, recoveries, and deaths across states, and another recording vaccination progress by dose, gender, and age group.

🎯 Problem Statements
1.Total Cases: Calculate the total confirmed COVID‑19 cases in India.
2.Active vs Recovered: Compare active cases against recovered cases across states.
3.Mortality Analysis: Study mortality rates (deaths ÷ confirmed cases) by state.
4.Recovery Rate: Compute average recovery rate nationally and state‑wise.
5.Top States by Active Cases: Visualize the 10 states with the highest active cases.
6.Top States by Deaths: Identify the 10 states with the highest death toll.
7.Growth Trends: Plot active case growth trends for the top 5 affected states.
8.Vaccination Progress: Track total doses administered over time across states.
9.Dose Distribution: Compare first vs second dose administration.
10.Gender Distribution: Visualize male vs female vaccination share.
11.State Vaccination Leaders: Identify states leading in vaccination coverage.
12.Correlation Study: Explore correlation between vaccination progress and active case decline.n

📊 Key Finding
( **Total Confirmed Cases: Maharashtra (~5.43M), Karnataka (~2.27M), Kerala (~2.20M)
Active vs Recovered: Delhi shows the highest recovery rate (~94.8%), while Uttarakhand is lowest (~72.5%)
Mortality Rate: Punjab records the highest (~2.4%), Kerala remains lowest (~0.3%)
Top States by Active Cases: Maharashtra, Karnataka, Kerala, Uttar Pradesh, Tamil Nadu
Top States by Deaths: Maharashtra (~83K), Karnataka (~22K), Delhi (~22K), Tamil Nadu (~18K)
Growth Trends: Maharashtra and Karnataka show sharp spikes during peak waves; Kerala’s growth is steadier
Vaccination Progress: Rollout began Jan 2021, scaling from ~48K doses to millions; Uttar Pradesh and Maharashtra lead in coverage
Gender Distribution: Vaccination split ~53% female vs ~47% male
Correlation Insight: States with higher vaccination coverage show declining active cases, suggesting a positive impact of vaccination
